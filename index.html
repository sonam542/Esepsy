Book Overview and Connection to My Goals

I chose The Science of Self-Discipline by Peter Hollins because I wanted to strengthen my ability to stay focused and consistent, especially with challenging tasks like coding assignments and sticking to a gym routine. The book offers actionable strategies to resist temptation, break bad habits, and push forward when motivation fades. These ideas directly supported the personal goals I set for this semester.

My Goals

I focused on two main goals for this Sementer:

1) Stay focused and avoid procrastination on coding assignments. I often get overwhelmed by complex programming tasks and end up procrastinating or avoiding them.

2) Maintain a consistent gym routine by working out at least four times a week. In the past, I struggled with consistency due to distractions or lack of motivation.

I chose these goals because they both require discipline, long-term effort, and the ability to push through discomfort and gain skills I wanted to improve.

Strategies I used

The 10-Minute Rule: This strategy helped me overcome resistance by telling myself to start a task for just 10 minutes. Once I started, I often ended up working longer. This was especially useful for getting started on difficult coding tasks.
